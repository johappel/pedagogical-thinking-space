# LEARNING_DESIGN.md

> *The shared thinking space between teacher and Critical Friend.*

---

# Purpose

The Learning Design is the shared object of every conversation.

The Critical Friend never discusses documents.

The teacher never designs files.

Both think together about a learning experience.

Everything else is derived from this shared understanding.

Workers create artefacts.

Renderers create outputs.

The Learning Design remains the same.

## Relationship to the other canonical artefacts

`learning-design.md` is the overarching pedagogical understanding.
`learning-landscape.md` records learning moments and transitions;
`temporal-plan.yml` records teaching windows and their placements;
`planning-board.yml` records work items; `decisions.yml` records reasoned
decisions; and `materials/` records drafts and reviewed outputs. An
application may project them together, but must not create a second canonical
pedagogical model.

---

# The Learning Design is not...

It is **not**

* a lesson plan,
* a worksheet,
* a LiaScript course,
* a RELIPULS article,
* a Moodle course,
* or a presentation.

These are all representations.

The Learning Design exists one level above them.

It captures educational intent rather than educational products.

---

# The central question

Every conversation revolves around one question:

> **What kind of learning experience are we trying to create?**

Not

> "What document are we writing?"

---

# Layers of a Learning Design

A Learning Design gradually evolves through reflection.

It is never completed from top to bottom.

Instead, teacher and Critical Friend continuously move between the different layers.

## 1. Context

Why does this learning experience exist?

* educational setting
* learners
* available time
* curriculum
* constraints
* opportunities

---

## 2. Educational Intention

What should change?

Not only

"What should learners know?"

but also

* understand
* question
* experience
* discover
* become able to do
* become aware of

---

## 3. Learning Journey

Learning is understood as a journey.

Questions include:

* Where do learners begin?
* Which misconceptions already exist?
* Which turning points should occur?
* Where will uncertainty arise?
* What should become clearer?
* What should remain open?

---

## 4. Learning Moments

Every journey consists of meaningful moments.

Examples:

* encountering a dilemma
* making a decision
* experiencing surprise
* collaborative exploration
* reflection
* discussion
* creation
* transfer

Methods serve these moments.

They never replace them.

---

## 5. Activities

Only now do concrete methods emerge.

Examples:

* Position Line
* Think-Pair-Share
* Experiment
* Role Play
* Debate
* Gallery Walk

Activities are always justified by the learning journey.

Never the other way around.

---

## 6. Materials

Materials support activities.

They are never the starting point.

Examples:

* texts
* images
* videos
* worksheets
* AI prompts
* simulations
* experiments

---

## 7. Reflection

Reflection happens on two levels.

Learners reflect on their learning.

Teachers reflect on the design itself.

Both become part of future experience.

---

# Design Decisions

Every important decision should remain visible.

Not only

"What did we decide?"

but also

"Why?"

Alternative ideas are not discarded.

They become part of the design history.

This allows future teachers — including our future selves — to understand how the design evolved.

---

# Open Questions

A Learning Design is never completely finished.

Open questions are valuable.

The Critical Friend deliberately keeps uncertainty visible until sufficient understanding has emerged.

---

# Relationship to Memory

Memory does not store documents.

Memory stores experiences that may influence the Learning Design.

Examples:

* similar lessons
* learner reactions
* successful approaches
* failed approaches
* personal reflections
* recurring patterns

The Learning Design decides when these memories become relevant.

---

# Relationship to Workers

Workers never invent educational decisions.

Workers implement existing decisions.

They always work from a snapshot of the current Learning Design.

---

# Relationship to Renderers

Renderers never redesign learning.

They only express the Learning Design in another form.

A single Learning Design may become

* a RELIPULS article,
* a LiaScript course,
* a Moodle course,
* a teacher guide,
* a workshop,
* or many other representations.

The design stays identical.

Only the representation changes.

---

# Principle

The Learning Design is the living centre of the project.

Everything begins here.

Everything returns here.
